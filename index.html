Why we exist

For readers tired of
the same old lists

Every "best books" recommendation feels identical. We built something different.

"Bookish exists because the best book you'll ever read might be completely unknown to most people — and you deserve to find it."

The problem we solve

AI book recommendations default to what's famous, what's sold the most, what's been discussed the longest. You keep getting The Great Gatsby when you asked for something new. Bookish is different — we actively surface lesser-known gems alongside acclaimed titles, filtered through your actual taste profile, not algorithmic popularity.

✦
Smart profiling

We learn your tone preferences, pacing, themes to avoid, and favourite authors to make every recommendation personal.

📚
Real book data

Powered by Amazon Books & Goodreads data — real covers, ratings, publication dates, and reader reviews.

🌹
Anti-algorithm

We don't weight by sales rank. A debut novel can appear alongside a Booker Prize winner if it fits your taste.

💬
Honest reasons

Every recommendation comes with a clear explanation: "because you loved X and enjoy Y tone…"

What makes a good recommendation?

We look at genre, sub-genre, narrative tone, pacing, protagonist type, themes, emotional register, prose style, and author voice. We cross-reference your favourites with books that share DNA — not just the same genre tag. And we actively filter out anything on your avoid list, including authors you've grown tired of.

English books, global authors

We recommend books in English — but that includes translations. If there's a stunning Italian novel, a Japanese literary gem, or a French prize-winner available in English translation, it belongs in your list. Great literature has no borders, only language access.
